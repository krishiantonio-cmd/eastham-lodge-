02

A new clubhouse changes everything on the policy.

Eastham Lodge's partnership with Prospect Capital brought a brand-new two-storey clubhouse with bars on both levels, views over the Mersey, and a completely different footprint to the original building. That's a significant change to the club's insurable profile — and one that standard commercial policies rarely account for properly.

We've seen this pattern before with clubs that have gone through major rebuilds. The old policy schedule rolls forward, the values don't get updated, and the club ends up either underinsured on the new building or paying for cover that was sized for the old one. Neither is ideal.

Questions worth asking before October

  • Has the buildings reinstatement value been fully re-assessed since the new clubhouse opened?
  • Are contents (bars, kitchen, furniture, AV equipment) properly scheduled at replacement cost?
  • What's the ownership split between the club and Prospect Capital — and who insures what?
  • Is the 70-apartment development next door creating any shared-boundary liability exposure?
  • Have the fragmented policies been consolidated to reflect the new single-site operation?
03

Where standard policies quietly leave gaps — especially for golf clubs.

Public liability and employer liability are the right starting point. But a members' golf club with a new clubhouse, visiting societies, and a function business is not just a business with a garden. Here's what tends to fall through the cracks on a standard commercial policy.

What standard cover often misses

  • Buildings reinstatement sized for the wrong clubhouse
  • Buggy fleet — often unspecified or undervalued
  • Course liability on competition & open days
  • Visiting society and external event liability
  • Greenkeeper plant & machinery, correctly valued
  • Gaps between fragmented policies from different insurers

What a bespoke golf scheme covers

  • Full buildings reinstatement at current rebuild cost
  • Full buggy fleet cover, correctly scheduled and valued
  • Public liability including competitions, open days & visitors
  • Visiting society and third-party event liability
  • Greenkeeper personal accident & equipment
  • One consolidated policy — no gaps between providers
